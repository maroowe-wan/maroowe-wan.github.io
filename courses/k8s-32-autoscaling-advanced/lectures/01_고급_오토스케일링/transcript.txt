at scale. So, in this video, let's talk&nbsp;&nbsp;
about autoscaling. Now, there are many different&nbsp;
controllers, some of them built-in and some that&nbsp;&nbsp;
you need to additionally install in your cluster.&nbsp;
For example, your online store may get a lot of&nbsp;&nbsp;
traffic during the day and almost no one using&nbsp;
it at night. So, in order to save on compute,&nbsp;&nbsp;
you can either manually adjust or have some&nbsp;

Another example would be a big data ELT pipeline&nbsp;

of compute when you're not running anything.&nbsp;
For example, to scale up your application to&nbsp;&nbsp;

A node can be a VM or a server in a data center.&nbsp;
If you don’t have enough nodes in your Kubernetes&nbsp;&nbsp;
cluster, you need to scale the cluster itself&nbsp;

Now, the controller ships with Kubernetes, but&nbsp;&nbsp;

Again, the most common approach would be to deploy&nbsp;&nbsp;

clusters are much more expensive in terms of&nbsp;&nbsp;
how much you pay for CPU and memory compared to&nbsp;

committing to the serverless approach, but it does&nbsp;
reduce the maintenance of your infrastructure.&nbsp;
