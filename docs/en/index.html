Kafka for Beginners

Starting from the fundamental question of why messaging and event streaming are needed, this beginner course covers core concepts — Topic, Partition, Offset, and Broker — along with Kafka's architecture, and finishes with a hands-on end-to-end exercise where you produce and consume messages directly using a Producer and Consumer.

Beginners and newcomers to Kafka who are familiar with basic server/networking concepts and simple CLI usage. Advanced topics such as replication, delivery guarantees, Kafka Connect, Streams, and operations are covered in the follow-up 'Kafka Intermediate' track. · 6 lectures

Kafka Intermediate

A continuation course for learners who have completed Kafka Basics, covering operations, reliability, integrations, and stream processing. You will design high availability and durability using replication and ISR, understand delivery guarantees such as at-least-once and exactly-once along with idempotent producers, control ordering guarantees and load distribution through partitioning strategies and custom partitioners, manage schema evolution with Schema Registry and Avro, integrate external systems without code using Kafka Connect, get started with stateful stream processing via Kafka Streams, and round out your practical skills with retention, log compaction, and essential operations and monitoring.

Learners who have completed Kafka Basics (messaging fundamentals, Topic/Partition/Offset/Broker, architecture/ZooKeeper vs KRaft, Producer basics/acks/keys, Consumer/Consumer Groups, local end-to-end lab). Intermediate learners looking to expand their skills in operations, reliability, integrations, and stream processing. · 7 lectures

Kubernetes Advanced

A top-tier deep-dive course for learners who have completed Kubernetes Intermediate 2. Covers extending the Kubernetes API with CRDs and Operators, enforcing policies with Admission Control, configuring advanced autoscaling with VPA, Cluster Autoscaler, and KEDA, automating deployments with GitOps (ArgoCD) and progressive delivery (Canary and Blue-Green), and mastering traffic management, mTLS, and observability with the Istio service mesh.

Learners who have completed Kubernetes Intermediate 2 (already familiar with RBAC, scheduling, NetworkPolicy, Helm, and monitoring) · 7 lectures

Kubernetes for Beginners

A beginner-friendly course that builds a solid foundation in Kubernetes — from understanding why container orchestration exists, to deploying real applications using YAML manifests.

Absolute beginners to Kubernetes who have a basic familiarity with containers and Docker · 5 lectures

Kubernetes Intermediate

Hands-on Kubernetes for practitioners who already know the basics. Covers configuration management, persistent storage, health checks, zero-downtime deployments, resource management, autoscaling, and Ingress-based traffic routing.

Learners who are already familiar with Kubernetes fundamentals — Pods, Deployments, Services, and YAML manifests — and want to take the next step toward production-grade operations and deployment reliability. · 5 lectures

Kubernetes Intermediate 2

An advanced operations course for learners who have completed Kubernetes Intermediate 1. Covers stateful workloads with StatefulSet, batch and node-level controllers (Job, CronJob, DaemonSet), Namespace-based multi-tenancy with ResourceQuota, RBAC access control, scheduling control with Affinity and Taints/Tolerations, NetworkPolicy traffic restrictions, Helm packaging, and cluster observability with Prometheus, Grafana, and log collection.

Learners who have completed Kubernetes Intermediate 1 (ConfigMap/Secret, PV/PVC, Probes, rolling updates and rollback, requests/limits and HPA, Ingress) and want to broaden their real-world cluster operations skills. · 8 lectures
